BACKGROUND INFORMATION

 

All Local Swimming Committees (LSC) follow the Standards for Certification of Officials. 

 

Questions about the certification standards are addressed in the LSC Officials Certification FAQ.

 

Officials join USA Swimming and are certified by Colorado Swimming

 

Required officials’ clinics including the associated test, the Athlete Protection Training (APT) course, Concussion Protocol Training (CPT) course, and the Background Check initiation process are all located in the USA Swimming University online portal. Completion of these requirements as well as the training and certification status of an individual are tracked in the Officials Tracking System (OTS). See the Officials Tracking System webpage for an explanation of how to access OTS and what information you can find in OTS.

 

All officiating tests are open book using the current version of the USA Swimming Rulebook

 

Please direct any questions you have on the certification or recertification process to the Officials Chair

 

 

BASIC STEPS TO BECOMING A NEW OFFICIAL

 

New officials start as either an Administrative Official or a Stroke and Turn Official.

 

To start the process of becoming a certified official, you must first register as a member of USA Swimming. You may initially register as an "apprentice official" or as a "non-athlete member" in the "official" category."

 

Following registration, you will need to complete the following requirements to certify as an official.

 

  • Complete the Foundations of Officiating sourse. 
  • Complete either the Stroke & Turn Certification course or the Administrative Official Certification course (to avoid the $45 non-member fee, first register as an apprentice official or non-athlete member in the officials' category) courses. 
  • Complete a Background Check
  • Complete Athlete Protection Training
  • Complete Concussion Protocol Training
  • Complete on-deck sessions shadowing a certified official (These are Colorado specific templates)

    • Stroke and Turn Certification Template
    • Chief Judge Certification Template
    • Starter Certification Template
    • Referee Certification Template
    • Administrative Official Certification Template

 

BASIC STEPS TO BECOMING A JUNIOR OFFICIAL

 

USA Swimming athletes, aged 16-17, can become Junior Stroke and Turn Judges. The athlete must be registered as a premium athlete member and complete the same on-deck training requirements as adult trainees. In addition, the athlete must complete the corresponding online Stroke & Turn Certification Course, Safe Sport for Youth Athletes (13-17) Training, and Concussion Protocol Training. Once the Junior Official turns 18, they must complete Athlete Protection Training and a Background Check.
